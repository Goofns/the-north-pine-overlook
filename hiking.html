On foot

Hiking Near Bailey, Colorado

From easy meadow loops to a 14,000-foot summit, the best trails near the cabin, with the local notes that make a mountain day go smoothly.

The North Pine Overlook  /  Hiking Near Bailey

One of the best things about staying above Bailey is how many trailheads sit within a short drive, from gentle family loops to a genuine 14,000-foot summit. Here are the hikes worth planning your days around, roughly easiest to hardest.

Staunton State Park

About fifteen to thirty minutes from the cabin, reached off US-285 at Shaffers Crossing and up Elk Creek Road, Staunton is the area's hiking centerpiece. It rises from grassy meadows around 8,100 feet to granite cliffs above 10,000, and the trail network has something for every pace.

  • Davis Ponds Loop is an easy, scenic warmup with calm water and good odds of wildlife, well suited to families and newer hikers.
  • Staunton Ranch Trail is a wide, well-graded path through forest and meadow, popular and pleasant year-round.
  • Elk Falls is the big objective: a long round trip out to one of the tallest waterfalls on the Front Range, best as a full-day outing. The Elk Falls Overlook is a shorter way to earn a view.

Staunton charges a daily vehicle entrance fee (around $10), and its closest parking lot fills early on summer weekends, so arrive in the morning or use the overflow lots.

Lost Creek Wilderness

South of town in Pike National Forest, Lost Creek is a different world: weathered granite domes and spires, hidden creeks, and open meadows, with far fewer people. Day hikes here are rewarding on their own, and it is a favorite for backpackers chasing solitude. Bring a map and plenty of water, since cell service is spotty.

Into the high country

North of Bailey, trails climb into the Mount Blue Sky Wilderness (formerly Mount Evans) and serious alpine terrain.

  • The Tanglewood Trail works up through aspen and pine into the wilderness, with wildflowers in summer and golden aspens in fall. Strong hikers can continue toward 13,575-foot Rosalie Peak for a big, uncrowded summit day.
  • The Deer Creek Trail is a popular route into the same high country, with good wildlife and steady elevation gain.

Guanella Pass and a 14er

About forty minutes away, Guanella Pass is the launch point for Mount Bierstadt (14,065 feet), one of Colorado's more approachable 14ers, plus gentler gems like the alpine Silver Dollar Lake. These high trailheads fill fast on summer weekends, so start at dawn and watch the forecast.

Close to the cabin

  • The Meridian Hill Trail near town climbs through shady forest to summit views over the Platte River Valley, ideal for a quick morning or a sunset walk.
  • Pine Valley Ranch Park, about twenty minutes away, has easy paths along the North Fork of the South Platte and connects to the longer Buffalo Creek trail system.
  • The Buffalo Creek Recreation Area is a renowned mountain-biking destination if you bring wheels.
Hike smart at altitude

You will be starting high and climbing higher, so take it slow your first day, drink more water than feels necessary, and use sunscreen even when it is cool. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer, so plan to be off exposed ridges and summits by early afternoon. Dogs are welcome on most local trails on leash, and packing out everything keeps these places wild.
