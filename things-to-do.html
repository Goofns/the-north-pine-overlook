Things to Do Near Bailey, Colorado

A practical guide to the trails, water, scenic drives, and small-town stops around Bailey and Platte Canyon.

The North Pine Overlook  /  Things to Do Near Bailey

Bailey sits on US Highway 285 in Platte Canyon near Pike National Forest and Lost Creek Wilderness. These are source-checked planning candidates, not guarantees or endorsements of current access. Check each destination's official site for current hours, fees, permits, parking, fire restrictions, and closures.

Planning around a specific weekend? We keep a running events calendar for Bailey and the 285 corridor.

Get out on the trails

Staunton State Park offers natural-surface trails from short outings to the 12-mile Elk Falls route. Pine Valley Ranch Park has shorter lake and trail options. South of Bailey lies Lost Creek Wilderness, where access must be tied to a named trailhead; to the north, the congressionally designated Mount Evans Wilderness includes Mount Blue Sky and Mount Bierstadt.

For the full rundown of trailheads and distances, see our hiking guide.

Cast a line

The North Fork of the South Platte has public and private stretches that require precise access planning; Pine Valley Ranch Park is one named public boundary. Wellington Lake and the ponds at Staunton are other managed options. Check current access, capacity, stocking information, fees, licenses, and water-specific rules before leaving; see our fishing guide for planning notes. Stocking and catches are never guaranteed.

Take a scenic drive

Scenic drives are one way to explore the area, with access and conditions changing by season.

  • Guanella Pass Scenic Byway is a 24-mile paved seasonal route between Georgetown and Grant, with access to the Bierstadt Trailhead for 14,060-foot Mount Bierstadt. Check the county's live road status before travel.
  • Kenosha Pass lies on US-285 and provides access to the Colorado Trail in both directions; check current road and Forest Service conditions before travel.
  • Mount Blue Sky Scenic Byway climbs 28 miles to 14,130 feet. During the operating season, vehicles and motorcycles need a timed reservation; check Recreation.gov and CDOT for seasonal openings, event closures, and weather closures before driving.

Explore the town

Bailey has more character than its size suggests.

  • The Coney Island Boardwalk, a diner shaped like a giant hot dog right on Highway 285, is a classic roadside stop.
  • The Sasquatch Outpost leans into local Bigfoot lore with a small museum and shop.
  • McGraw Memorial Park preserves Bailey-area history. Its historic buildings operate seasonally; check the Park County Historical Society for current open dates or appointments.
  • In the Bailey area, Aspen Peak Cellars and Craft Mountain Brewing are possible stops; check each business's current hours and offerings before visiting.
Give wildlife room

Bighorn sheep and other wildlife may be seen around Platte Canyon, but sightings are never guaranteed. Watch the road, keep a safe distance, and never approach or feed wildlife.

Take a day trip

South Park City Museum in Fairplay interprets a 19th-century mining town through more than 40 historic buildings and collections. It operates seasonally, so check current dates and hours before leaving.

Good in every season

Summer supports long days outdoors, fall can bring changing aspens, winter shifts recreation toward snow travel, and spring conditions vary as trails reopen. Nearby destinations are independently managed, and access, capacity, fees, hours, permits, parking, fire restrictions, and seasonal conditions can change. Check the linked official source for the actual date of a trip. Wildlife sightings, fishing success, weather, road conditions, and parking are never guaranteed.
