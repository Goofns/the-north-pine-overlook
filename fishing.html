Fly Fishing Near Bailey, Colorado

Verified access notes for public water, stocked ponds, and privately operated fishing options around the Bailey area.

The North Pine Overlook  /  Fly Fishing Near Bailey

The South Platte system offers several fishing options near Bailey, but access, flows, closures, and regulations vary by water and segment. Check each destination's official source and Colorado Parks and Wildlife's current fishing guidance before leaving.

The North Fork of the South Platte

Jefferson County Open Space allows fishing, and wading only for fishing, on the North Fork within Pine Valley Ranch Park. The park operates from one hour before sunrise until one hour after sunset. Use the current official park map, remain inside public boundaries, and obey posted private-property signs and current CPW rules. No authoritative stocking schedule was found for this segment, so do not assume it was recently stocked.

Wellington Lake

Wellington Lake is a privately owned, capacity-limited recreation facility south of Bailey. Under the current operator rules, no Colorado fishing license is required; fishing is catch-and-release with artificial flies or lures, one barbless hook, no bait or scented plastics, and no lead fishing materials. Registration, access, fees, rules, and closures can change, so check the current fishing rules and same-day alerts before traveling. The operator recommends CR 68/CR 105 because Forest-road shortcuts are not maintained year-round.

A beginner-friendly option

At Davis Ponds in Staunton State Park, fishing is allowed and CPW publishes a May-through-September monthly rainbow-trout stocking schedule. A schedule is not proof of a specific recent stocking. Anglers 16 and older generally need a current Colorado fishing license, subject to CPW's current exemptions, and visitors need the applicable park pass. The park has an ADA-accessible fishing pier; contact the park at least two days ahead for weekday assistance.

A bigger day on the Platte

For a longer outing, the South Platte main stem near Deckers and Cheesman Canyon includes public fishing areas and private stretches. Tackle and harvest rules vary by exact segment, so the entire river must not be described as fly-only or catch-and-release. Consult CPW's current fishing regulations, the exact managed site's information, and posted signs.

Check stocking without promising fish

CPW's stocking report lists recent stocking activity during the fishing season. A water's absence does not prove that it was not stocked, and a manager's schedule does not guarantee fish, access, or a catch. Check the exact water and current regulations before every outing.

Before you go

For Colorado public waters, check CPW's current license requirements: youth 15 and under may fish without a license, while anglers 16 and older generally need the appropriate license unless an exemption applies. Private-water requirements can differ. Check water-specific rules and current closures before every trip. In warm weather, CPW advises moving elsewhere when water reaches 70°F and releasing fish quickly while keeping them submerged. Fish, access, water conditions, and catches are never guaranteed.
