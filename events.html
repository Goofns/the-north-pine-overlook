Events Near Bailey, Colorado

Festivals, live music, fairs, and state park programs around Bailey and the 285 corridor, kept current so you can plan around them.

The North Pine Overlook  /  Events Near Bailey

One of the good things about a small mountain town is that when something is happening, the whole canyon shows up. This is a running list of what is coming up around Bailey and along the Highway 285 corridor, from summer festivals and live music to state park programs and the fall color rush. We keep it current, and we take things off once they have passed.

Coming up

Most summer weekends

Live music at Aspen Peak Cellars. The winery just up US 285 in Bailey brings in local musicians on weekend afternoons through the warm months, out on the barrel stage when the weather cooperates. There is no cover and the kitchen is open, so it makes an easy, low-key stop on a Saturday or Sunday. Check their calendar for who is playing before you head over.

Source: Aspen Peak Cellars
July 28 to 30, and again August 11 to 13

Junior Rangers at Staunton State Park. A hands-on outdoor program for kids at Staunton, about fifteen minutes from the cabin, with climbing, fishing, archery, and hiking across three mornings. Registration is required and spots are limited, so sign up ahead through Colorado Parks and Wildlife rather than showing up. A good option if you are traveling with children.

Source: Colorado Parks and Wildlife
Labor Day weekend, early September

Bailey Fest. The town's big one. Live music across several days in Bailey, with camping on site and a crowd that comes in from all over the Front Range. If you are staying that weekend, plan around it rather than through it, since lodging in the canyon fills up.

Source: Explore Park County
Mid to late September

Aspen season on Kenosha Pass. Not an event exactly, but the biggest thing on the local calendar. The aspen stands along US 285 at Kenosha Pass turn gold, usually peaking mid to late September, and weekend traffic on the pass gets heavy. Weekday mornings are the quiet way to see it.

Source: US Forest Service, Pike San Isabel

Where these come from

We pull from Explore Park County, the Platte Canyon area chamber, Colorado Parks and Wildlife program listings for Staunton State Park, and the local places that host things: Aspen Peak Cellars, Mad Jack's Mountain Brewery, the Sasquatch Outpost, and the Park County Fair. Dates and details change, especially for small-town events, so treat this as a starting point and confirm with the organizer before you build a day around it.

Planning around the calendar

Two patterns worth knowing. Summer weekends bring festivals and live music, and the canyon gets busy. Mid to late September is aspen season, the single busiest stretch of the year on the 285 corridor, so book early and drive the passes on weekday mornings if you can. Winter is quiet by comparison, which for a lot of people is the point.

Staying nearby
