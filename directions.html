Directions to the Cabin

How to reach the cabin from Denver, plus drive times and a few notes that make the mountain stretch easy.

The North Pine Overlook  /  Directions to the Cabin

The North Pine Overlook sits in the pines above Bailey, in Platte Canyon, about an hour southwest of Denver. The drive is straightforward and scenic. Here is how to find us, plus a few notes that make the mountain stretch go smoothly.

From Denver

From the Denver metro, make your way to US Highway 285 heading southwest (most people pick it up via C-470 or Hampden Avenue). Stay on 285 as it climbs into the foothills and drops into Platte Canyon. After about 45 miles, roughly an hour of driving, you reach Bailey.

The final stretch

From Bailey, the cabin is a short drive off the highway.

Once your stay is confirmed, we send exact door-to-door directions, the entry details, and a few landmarks to watch for.

Notes for the mountain drive

  • GPS can drop in the canyon. Screenshot your directions or download an offline map before you leave the city, so a lost signal never leaves you guessing.
  • Winter means real mountain roads. Snow and ice are common from late fall into spring. All-wheel or four-wheel drive is a good idea, and it is worth checking road and weather conditions before you head up.
  • Watch for wildlife. Deer and bighorn sheep cross these roads, especially at dawn and dusk, so take the curves easy.
  • Fuel and groceries. It is smart to fill the tank and grab any supplies before you leave the metro, since options thin out the higher you go.

How far is everything?

Rough drive times from the cabin:

  • Denver: about 1 hour
  • Red Rocks Amphitheatre: about 45 minutes
  • Staunton State Park: 15 to 30 minutes
  • Wellington Lake: about 40 minutes
  • Guanella Pass and Mount Bierstadt: about 40 minutes
