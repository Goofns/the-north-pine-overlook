The setting

In the pines above Platte Canyon.

The North Pine Overlook sits among the ponderosa at around 7,750 feet, in the Front Range foothills where Bailey tucks into the canyon. Pike National Forest runs right up to the trees, and the North Fork of the South Platte threads through the valley below.

It's the kind of quiet that's hard to find within an hour of a city — long mornings, tall pines, and a herd of bighorn sheep that work the ridges nearby. Denver is about an hour east on Highway 285; most everything else is wilderness.

The area

An hour from the city, surrounded by the forest.

Pike National Forest

Over a million acres of pine and granite begin at the treeline — trails, old logging roads, and quiet you can walk straight into.

Fly the South Platte

The North Fork runs through the canyon below, holding wild brown and rainbow trout — some of the closest fly water to Denver.

Trails & state parks

Footpaths into the forest, with Staunton State Park and the Lost Creek Wilderness — cliffs, waterfalls, and high-country miles — a short drive away.

An hour from Denver

Close enough for a Friday-night drive up 285, far enough that the city feels like another world by the time you arrive.
