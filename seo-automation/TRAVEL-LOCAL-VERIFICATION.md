## Fishing verification

- Colorado public waters generally require a current license for anglers 16 and older; youth 15 and under may fish without one. Check current exemptions, fees, stamps, and dates on the [CPW license page](https://cpw.state.co.us/activities/fishing/fishing-licenses-and-dates).
- The [CPW stocking report](https://cpw.state.co.us/activities/fishing/fishing-awards-and-records/fish-stocking-report) is updated during fishing season and reports recent stocking activity; absence from the table does not prove a water was not stocked, and a published schedule does not prove a specific recent stocking.
- No authoritative stocking schedule was found for the North Fork at Pine Valley Ranch. Do not call it stocked.
- CPW states that Davis Ponds are scheduled for monthly rainbow-trout stocking from May through September. Describe that as the manager's schedule, not a guarantee of fish or recent stocking.
- Wellington Lake's operator says the private lake is stocked twice yearly but does not publish current stocking dates. No Colorado license is required there under current operator rules; fishing is catch-and-release with artificial flies/lures, a single barbless hook, no bait, no scented plastics, and no lead fishing materials.
- South Platte tackle and harvest rules vary by exact segment. Link the current regulations and never describe the entire river as fly-only or catch-and-release.
- Check [CPW's current fishing and closure guidance](https://cpw.state.co.us/fishing) on the travel date. Fish, stocking, access, water temperature, and catches are never guaranteed.

## Source-backed owner road wording awaiting approval

> Mountain road conditions and travel times can change without notice. Use the route provided in the property's arrival instructions and avoid mapping-app shortcuts. Before departing, check COtrip for US-285 conditions and active traction or chain restrictions, then check Park County and destination-specific notices. County roads, Forest Service roads, private roads, and the property driveway may receive different levels of maintenance. Access, pavement conditions, travel time, and any particular vehicle's ability to complete a trip are not guaranteed.

Owner must still confirm whether the final approach is county-maintained or private, who plows it, driveway grade and turnaround conditions, and whether AWD/4WD or traction equipment is recommended for the property itself.
